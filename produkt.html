HARVESTING

Cut the mushroom off the tree and discard the bottom part of the stem. Only the younger oyster mushrooms are worth taking home.

HARVESTING LOCATION

The common oyster mushroom grows on dead and dying hardwood trees - very often beech trees, but willow and poplar trees can also host oyster mushrooms. In some cases, you may even find them on coniferous trees.

SEASON

Common oyster mushroom is a winter fungus and can survive even relatively harsh periods of frost.

DESCRIPTION

Both the shape and color of an oyster mushroom can be compared to an oyster - harmonious and fan-shaped. In terms of color, the oyster mushroom ranges from steel gray to more brownish or dove-blue shades. The cap can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, smooth, and slightly shiny. The gills are creamy white or gray-blue, close together, and run down the stem for a distance. The stem is two to three cm long, white, and smooth. Oyster mushrooms often grow in clusters.
